Cities, towns and villages

city
population 7 million		 town
population 100,000		 village
population 400

Do you know the word population?  It means the number of people who live in a place.

Task 1: What's the population of the place where you live?

Under 2000: you live in a village.
More than 2000 but less than 1 million: you live in a town.
Over 1 million: you live in a city.


Task 2: Now read this text.  Use your dictionary for any words you do not understand.

I was born in a small village in the south of France.
I took the school bus from my village to the next town because my village was too small to have a school. There are only about 300 people there!
When I was 12, my parents moved to a small town because my father got a new job.
I went to school in the town but the town was too small to have a university, so I went to Marseilles to study when I finished school. Marseilles is not a very big city (with a little more than one million people) but for a small-town boy like me, it seemed very big. On my first day, I went for a walk and got lost in the old streets near the sea.
I have been living in Marseilles for two years now and I don't get lost any longer but it still feels very big to me. I live a long way from the university so I have to take three buses to get to my studies every day. When I lived in a town, I walked to school in 10 minutes!
But I like living in a city because there are so many things to do. In Marseilles we have lots of cinemas and theatres as well as some of the best shops and cafes in France. I love to sit in a cafe in the city centre with my friends from university and talk.
In my village, there was nothing to do in the evenings and there wasn't even a shop. We drove 10 kilometres every week to do the shopping. In the town, there was only one small cinema and no theatre or bookshop. In fact, there were only about 20 shops there.
Marseilles is better than that and there are shops everywhere.
When I finish university, I want to get a job in Marseilles. I love it.

Task 4: Now read this text about New York City.

The City of New York, often called New York City (NYC), is the biggest city in the United States. The population is over 8.5 million.
New York is home to the headquarters of the United Nations.
New York City has five boroughs – Brooklyn, Queens, Manhattan, The Bronx, and Staten Island and they were put together in 1898 to make New York City.
800 languages are spoken in New York, more than any other city in the world.
3.2 million of New York's people were born outside the United States.
New York City was founded by colonists from the Dutch Republic in 1624 when it was called New Amsterdam.
The English took over the city 1664 and named it New York.
New York was the capital of the United States from 1785 until 1790 and has been the country's largest city since 1790.
The city received 62.8 million tourists in 2017.
The names of many of the city's landmarks, skyscrapers, and parks are known around the world, for example, The Statue of Liberty, The Empire State Building, Times Square and Central Park.
The New York City Subway has 472 rail stations.
In New York, there are over 120 colleges and universities, including Columbia University, New York University, and Rockefeller University, which are among the top universities in the world.

Task 6: Now read this text about a village in Greece.

Vasilitsi is a small Greek village, really, with just 500 or so people but it is a working village. Most of the people here are farmers and they grow olives for oil and grapes for wine.
The village sits on top of a hill about 3 kilometers from the sea and it is not on the main road so it's very quiet.
The village has five coffee shops and one very good restaurant. As well as that, there's a mini-market which sells almost everything you want. People come from all over the area to eat at the restaurant because the food's so good.
The village has a big, beautiful church and lots of people come to it on special occasions to get married and so on. It's too big for such a small village really.
Until 6 years ago, the village had its own school but that closed and now the children have to go to the nearest town to school. It's only 8 kilometers from here and the bus takes them in the morning and brings them back in the afternoon. There's no doctor but one visits every Wednesday and he has an office in the village.
A bus service connects the village with the local towns and shops or you can take the school but in the morning (but it's very noisy).

Task 8: Now read this text about a town in England.  Use the pictures to help you understand.

Eastbourne is a seaside town in East Sussex on the south coast of England, 60 miles (96 kilometers) south of London. Eastbourne is immediately to the east of Beachy Head, the highest chalk sea cliff in Great Britain.
Eastbourne grew from 4 small villages in 1859 to a popular holiday resort so its really quite a new town.
As a seaside resort Eastbourne gets a lot of money from tourists and there is also a large shopping centre in the middle of the town.
Eastbourne has four cinemas, two theatres, an art gallery and a new hospital, too, and the population is getting bigger all the time with younger people moving in. Now, the population is close to 100,000 and growing.
There are lots of schools in Eastbourne, both private and state, and some of the University of Brighton has departments in the town.
The buildings on the seafront in Eastbourne are mostly hotels because no shops are allowed there. The beach is not sandy, it's stony, but that doesn't stop hundreds of people sitting on it in the summer and swimming in the sea.

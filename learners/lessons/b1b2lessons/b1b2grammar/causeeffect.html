Cause and effect

→
the cause and the effect
Lighting the wood started the fire

When one thing makes another thing happen, we can join the two with some special words (conjunctions).  In this lesson we look at these:

so
this joins two sentences, like this:
Cause Effect
It was raining so she took her umbrella
Sentence Sentence
because
this joins two sentences, like this:
Cause Effect
Because it was very cold she wore her gloves
Sentence + Sentence
or like this:
Effect Cause
She wore her gloves because it was very cold
Sentence Sentence
because of
joins a noun to a sentence, like this:
Cause Effect
Because of the storm her plane was late
Noun + Sentence
or like this:
Effect Cause
Her plane was late because of the storm
Sentence Noun
owing to
joins a noun to a sentence, like this:
Cause Effect
Owing to the snow he was late to work
Noun + Sentence
or like this
Effect Cause
He was late to work owing to the snow
Sentence Noun
due to
joins a noun to a sentence, like this:
Cause Effect
Due to the rain the road was closed
Noun + Sentence
or like this
Effect Cause
The road was closed due to the rain
Sentence Noun
Task 1: What is the rule?
How do we use these words.  Look again at the examples and write three rules.
Click here when you have done that.

The rules
  1. The word so can only come between two sentences:
    It was late so I went home is correct.
    So I went home, it was late is wrong!
  2. When we use so, the cause comes first:
    It was raining so I took my coat is correct.
    I took my coat so it was raining is wrong!
  3. The words so and because join two sentences:
    I was late so I hurried is correct.
    I hurried because I was late is correct.
    I took my coat because the rain is wrong!
    So the rain I took my coat is wrong!
  4. The words because of, owing to and due to join a noun to a sentence.
    We can have the cause first or the effect first.
    Owing to the bad weather, the train was late is correct.
    Due to the rain we did not go to the beach is correct.
    The train was late owing to the bad weather is correct.
    We did not go to the beach owing to the bad weather is correct.
    Owing to it was raining we did not go to the beach is wrong!
    Due to the weather was bad the train was late is wrong!

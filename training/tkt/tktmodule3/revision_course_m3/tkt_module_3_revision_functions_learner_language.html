Identifying the functions of learners' language

Click here to go to this section of the course.

Make a note of your answer to the question and then click on the to reveal the answer.

Identify three language functions learners need to be able to perform with the teacher.
Give examples of what you mean.
With the teacher, learners need at least to be able to:
Ask questions about meaning, form and pronunciation
   How do I say this word?
   What does 'suggestive' mean here?
   Do you say 'everyone like' or 'everyone likes'?
Ask for clarification
   Do we always use the -ing form with this verb?
Ask for repetition
   Could you repeat that, please?
Ask for permission
   Can I go and get some more paper, please?
Express doubt
   I'm not sure I understand the task.
Apologise
   I'm sorry I'm late.
Explain
   My bus didn't arrive.
   I know the word but I don't know what it means in this sentence.
Identify three language functions learners need to be able to perform with other learners.
Give example of what you mean.
With each other, learners need to be able to:
Tell (i.e., give information)
   The past tense is came not come.
Explain
   We have to find the words in the text that mean the same as these phrases here.
Ask each other what they think
  Do you think we should put the house here?
Say what they think
   I don't think that's right.  We need a different adjective.
Agree and Disagree
   Yes, I think that right.
   I'm not sure that's right.
   That's wrong, I think.
Encourage and motivate
   Right, let's start.  This is not too difficult.
What is a class callout?
Give an example of what you mean.
A class callout is a simple way to present classroom language and leave it on the classroom walls as a permanent reminder for the learners.
Examples are:

That's the end.

How did you do?

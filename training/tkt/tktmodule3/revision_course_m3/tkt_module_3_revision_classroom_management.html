There are three questions to ask when considering the layout of the room.
Can you remember two of them?
The three questions were:
Can everyone see critical aids: the whiteboard, the screen, the flip chart etc.?
Can you see everyone and can everyone see you?
Has everyone got somewhere to write and take notes when it is necessary?
Why is it important to have settled systems and rules?
Having clear systems and rules helps our learners feel comfortable and allows them to focus on learning without the distraction of a changing environment.
Give two examples of what may be in a class contract, one on the learners' side, one on the teacher's side.
From the learners' side, examples are:
   We will always try to speak in English
   We will not be late
   We will respect each other
   We will bring our books and writing stuff
From the teacher's side, example are:
   I will prepare all the lessons carefully
   I will always try to help and support you
   I will never laugh at your English
Give three examples of things you can do to get variety into lessons.
Changing groupings
Not allowing people to sit where they usually do
Using a range of materials
Using a variety of lesson structures
Varying assessment procedures and tasks

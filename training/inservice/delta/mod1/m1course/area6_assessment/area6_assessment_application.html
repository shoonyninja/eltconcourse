Delta Module One: Syllabus area 6
Key concepts and terminology related to assessment
Examination tasks

Your knowledge of assessment concepts and terminology is specifically tested in Paper 2, Task 1 of the examination.
That task carries 18% of the possible marks for the paper so don't get too bogged down in all the terminology at the expense of focusing on more productive areas.
Tasks Examples

Paper 2, Task 1

In this task you will be presented with a test or an extract from one and be asked to comment on it in terms of its effectiveness for the stated use.
Note 'for the stated use' in that line.  You are not being asked to evaluate it generally but in terms of what it is intended to do.
A perfectly acceptable test of, say, the ability to write an email to a friend will be useless if it is actually spoken accuracy that you want to test.

 Cambridge is in the business of testing and evaluation so the examination setters have a large database of possible test-types to draw on.  It's not practical to cover all of them here.
By now, you should be able to recognise and describe the test type and its probable marking system.  You need to show you can do this and then refer to key concepts to comment on their fitness for the stated purposes.
Here's a selection.  Click on the table when you have written your comments.

Look now at a selection of tests and examination items / tasks you know and decide on their validity, reliability and practicality.
What about this task in the Delta examination?  How does it match up to the criteria?

Some more ideas to prepare for this area of the syllabus:

  • The guide to Paper 2 contains a few more examples of the sorts of things you may be presented with for this question.
  • Select any class you currently teach and design a progress test taking into account issues of validity, practicality and reliability.  Doing this is a good way to alert you to what is required in this area and how you can form a critical view of a test format and the test items.
  • Go through the guide to assessment and testing again, noting down all the technical terms it contains.  Shut the page and then try to write down:
    • A definition of the terms
    • Whether the terms refer to testing generally, to marking or to test item types
    • What relevance the terms have for validity and reliability
  • When you are ready, do the Mock Examination for Delta Module One.
    Do NOT do this until you have worked through all the materials in this course or you will be putting the cart before the horse and wasting the mock examination before you are ready to benefit from it.

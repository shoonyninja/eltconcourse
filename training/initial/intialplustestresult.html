The Initial Plus Grammar Test Result

How did you do?

Score Comment
Below 25% That's quite poor so you need to do two things quite soon:
  1. Get hold of two types of reference books:
    1. a grammar for students such as Michael Swan's Practical English Usage
    2. a reference grammar for you to learn more than your students need to know
      There is a list of both of these sorts of grammars on this site.
      You also need to read them, of course.  Not all at once; a chapter at a time.
  2. Go through the grammar guides on this site in the initial plus section.  Doing one every day or two will be very helpful.
If you don't know the difference between a subject and an object or your complement from your elbow, it may be wise to follow the basic ELT training course grammar section which introduces some fundamental concepts.
There is also a short language analysis course on this site which is slightly more challenging.
26% to 50% Not too bad at all, especially if you haven't yet taken a training course.  You need to focus on the questions you got wrong and then follow the guides to those areas in the initial plus training section.
51% to 80% Well done.  Some of the questions were quite hard.  Follow the guides in the initial plus training section to the areas you had trouble with and then move on to the guides in the in-service training section.
Over 80% You don't need much help so now you are ready to go to the guides in the in-service training section.
